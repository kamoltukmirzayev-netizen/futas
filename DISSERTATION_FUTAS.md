# Technical Description of the FUTAS System for the Dissertation

*Prepared in the academic (OAK/ВАК) style as a ready section of the dissertation.*

**Dissertation topic:** “Technical analysis methods of international trade and
trading strategies of the digital economy and cryptocurrency (based on
cryptocurrency and gold assets).”
**Specialization:** 08.00.16 — Digital economy and international digital integration.

---

## 1. Relevance of the research

The digitalisation of international trade has turned cryptocurrencies and gold
into globally traded, around‑the‑clock digital assets whose price dynamics are
formed in an integrated, cross‑border information environment. Classical
technical‑analysis instruments — in particular the fixed Fibonacci retracement
ratios — were formulated for traditional markets and do not adapt to the
heightened volatility and structural breaks characteristic of digital‑economy
assets. This creates a scientific and practical need for a formalised,
reproducible and adaptive method of price‑structure analysis. The present
research answers this need by proposing and software‑implementing the
**Fibonacci Urvin Adaptive Trading Analysis System (FUTAS)**.

## 2. Object and subject of the research

* **Object:** the price behaviour of international digital‑economy assets
  (cryptocurrency and gold) within the system of international digital
  integration.
* **Subject:** the methods, models and algorithms of technical analysis that
  formalise the identification of market structure and the construction of
  adaptive price levels and trading set‑ups for these assets.

## 3. Aim and tasks

**Aim:** to develop and verify an adaptive, fully reproducible method of
technical analysis — and its software realisation as a web application — that
derives complete trading set‑ups (entry, protective and target levels) solely
from a single mathematical model, without recourse to expert‑set or historical
values.

**Tasks:**
1. formalise an adaptive coefficient system replacing the classical Fibonacci
   ratios;
2. define a single linear projection model generating all price levels;
3. formalise the detection of trend, market structure and market phase;
4. integrate the structural analysis with the adaptive levels to obtain
   BUY / SELL / WAIT decisions with risk management;
5. implement the method as a published web application with import, export and
   verification facilities;
6. verify the method by deterministic testing and walk‑forward backtesting.

## 4. Scientific novelty

1. A **Fibonacci Urvin adaptive coefficient system** is introduced — a fixed
   set of **15 coefficients** that generalises and replaces the classical
   Fibonacci retracement ratios for digital‑economy assets:

   ```
   K ∈ { 1.0, 0.0, 0.5, 0.5993, −0.6993, 1.5993, −0.5993, 1.1987,
         1.6987, 1.7973, −0.1987, −0.0987, −0.7973, 0.3973, 1.0993 }
   ```

2. A **single linear projection model** is proposed by which the entire system
   of analytical levels is generated:

   ```
   P = Low + (High − Low) × K
   ```

   where `P` is the calculated price level, `High` and `Low` are the extrema of
   the analysed range, and `K` is an adaptive coefficient. Coefficients with
   `0 ≤ K ≤ 1` describe internal retracement levels, `K > 1` upward extensions,
   and `K < 0` downward extensions.

3. A **closed‑loop decision rule** is formalised in which the entry is taken
   from the live price and the Stop‑Loss and all Take‑Profit levels are
   *selected only* from the 15 calculated levels — eliminating expert‑set,
   hard‑coded or historically borrowed values from the trading logic.

4. The structural analysis (swing detection, HH/HL/LH/LL labelling, Break of
   Structure and Change of Character) is **integrated with the adaptive levels**
   into a single reproducible signal‑generation procedure with quantified
   Risk/Reward and confidence.

## 5. Methodology and the formal model

The method proceeds in deterministic stages. From the input series the dominant
range `[Low, High]` is identified and the **fifteen levels** are computed by the
projection `P = Low + (High − Low) × K`. Swing points are detected as fractals;
the trend (UPTREND / DOWNTREND / SIDEWAY) is established by reconciling a
market‑structure verdict (two consecutive higher‑highs and higher‑lows, or their
mirror) with the normalised slope of a linear regression of closing prices, a
conflict between the two being resolved conservatively as a sideways state. The
market structure is labelled and its turning points (BOS, CHoCH) extracted; the
current leg is classified as **impulse** or **correction**.

A signal is then generated by integrating the directional context with the
levels. For a bullish context in which price rests on or retraces to an adaptive
support, a **BUY** is issued with

* **Entry** `E` = current price;
* **Stop‑Loss** `SL` = the nearest adaptive level beyond the support;
* **Take‑Profits** `TP₁, TP₂, TP₃` = the next adaptive levels in the trade
  direction;
* **Risk/Reward** `RRᵢ = (TPᵢ − E) / (E − SL)`.

The **SELL** case is symmetric. If no admissible Stop‑Loss or Take‑Profit exists
among the levels, or if `RR₁` is below a set minimum, the set‑up is downgraded to
**WAIT**. Because `SL` and every `TPᵢ` are drawn exclusively from the fifteen
values `{ Low + (High − Low)·K }`, each protective and target price is, by
construction, a member of the adaptive level set.

## 6. Software implementation

The method is realised as a **web application** (Python; Streamlit) with a
separable scientific core:

* `futas_engine.py` — the analytical core (levels, structure, trend, phase,
  signal, risk management, scientific explanation, walk‑forward backtest);
* `ocr_ingest.py` — ingestion of OHLC data from a **screenshot or photo** of a
  trading panel (optical character recognition), in addition to CSV, manual and
  pasted‑text input;
* `app_streamlit.py` — the user interface (interactive candlestick charts with
  the level overlay, signal and risk panels, backtest report, and export to
  Excel, text and CSV);
* a spreadsheet template (`FUTAS_Excel_Template.xlsx`) reproducing the model in
  auditable Excel formulas.

The coefficient set is stored as an immutable signature, so that any published
build can be proven to use the unmodified research coefficients — guaranteeing
the reproducibility required of a scientific result. The application is prepared
for public deployment (Streamlit Community Cloud), enabling the cited use of the
instrument in the dissertation defence.

## 7. Results and verification

The implementation was verified to satisfy the model exactly: the fifteen levels
reproduce `P = Low + (High − Low) × K` to within numerical precision, and the
Stop‑Loss and all Take‑Profit values are confirmed, in every generated signal,
to be members of the fifteen adaptive levels. On structured demonstrative data
for gold (XAUUSD) the system produced a complete **BUY** set‑up with entry,
adaptive Stop‑Loss and three adaptive Take‑Profit targets with increasing
Risk/Reward; for bitcoin (BTCUSD) it produced a symmetric **SELL** set‑up. A
walk‑forward backtest, recomputing the levels and the signal on a rolling window
without look‑ahead, supplies objective statistics (win rate, profit factor,
maximum drawdown, equity curve); on unstructured (random) series the same
backtest reports weak performance, confirming that the procedure is not
over‑fitted and that its analytical value derives from genuine market structure.

## 8. Practical significance and statements for defence

The results are directly applicable to the formalised analysis of
digital‑economy assets in the system of international digital integration and to
the reproducible teaching and study of technical‑analysis methods.

**Statements submitted for defence:**
1. the Fibonacci Urvin adaptive coefficient system (fifteen coefficients) as a
   generalisation of the classical Fibonacci ratios for digital‑economy assets;
2. the single linear projection model `P = Low + (High − Low) × K` generating
   the complete system of analytical levels;
3. the closed‑loop decision rule in which the entry is the live price and all
   protective and target levels are selected only from the calculated levels;
4. the integrated, reproducible software realisation (web application) of the
   method, with import from images, export of reports, and walk‑forward
   verification.

## 9. Conclusions

The research proposes, formalises and software‑implements an adaptive method of
technical analysis for international digital‑economy assets. Its scientific
contribution is the replacement of fixed Fibonacci ratios by an adaptive
fifteen‑coefficient system and a single projection model from which every
analytical and risk‑management level is reproducibly derived. The accompanying
web application makes the method auditable, reproducible and publishable.
